**Today's Feature Ideas — April 14, 2026**

---

1. **Step Read-Aloud via Browser Speech API** (Section 11)
   - What: Add a speaker icon to guided cook StepCard that reads the current instruction aloud using the Web Speech API — already spec'd as Section 11.1.
   - Why now: The [SmartVoice Air Fryer](https://www.foodnetwork.com/how-to/packages/shopping/best-ces-2026-kitchen-products) launched with 1,000+ voice commands and zero app dependency, signaling that voice-in-kitchen is crossing from novelty to baseline expectation. Sous users with wet/messy hands during the Cook phase are the exact use case.
   - Effort: Low — browser Speech API, single icon toggle, no new screen
   - Impact on cooks-per-week: Medium-high. Removes the friction of stopping to read, reducing cook abandonment mid-flow. Hands-free progression keeps users in the guided cook instead of reaching for their phone.
   - Recommendation: **Build** — lowest-effort Section 11 candidate with the strongest market tailwind right now.

2. **Progressive Difficulty Bias in Quest Suggestions** (Section 11)
   - What: After 10+ completed cooks, silently weight quest card suggestions toward dishes one skill tier above the user's comfort zone — already spec'd as Section 11.7. Pure engine logic, zero UI change.
   - Why now: The [CES 2026 trend toward "predictive, guided cooking"](https://developex.com/blog/smart-kitchen-devices-software-2026/) (e.g., Wan AIChef Ultra's adaptive recipe personalization) is raising user expectations for apps that grow with them. Sous's static quest pool will feel flat against competitors that adapt to skill level.
   - Effort: Low — scorer weight adjustment in the existing pairing engine, no UI work
   - Impact on cooks-per-week: Medium. Prevents plateau boredom after the first 2-3 weeks. Users who feel they're progressing cook more consistently than those stuck at the same difficulty.
   - Recommendation: **Build** — invisible to users, strengthens both Data and Engine moats, and directly addresses Week-4 retention risk (Strategy §9.4).

3. **Offline Guided Cook via PWA Service Worker** (Section 12)
   - What: Cache guided cook step data (text + timers) for offline use so the Cook phase works in kitchens with poor WiFi — already spec'd as Section 12.8 with status "UNDER CONSIDERATION."
   - Why now: The [SmartVoice Air Fryer's fully offline, no-account design](https://www.foodnetwork.com/how-to/packages/shopping/best-ces-2026-kitchen-products) highlights that reliable kitchen connectivity is a luxury. Sous's guided cook is the highest-stakes moment — a WiFi dropout mid-step could cause a burned dish and a lost user. The broader [food-tech push toward embedded, offline-capable systems](https://www.vml.com/insight/ces-2026-autonomous-kitchens) makes this a growing user expectation.
   - Effort: Medium — service worker setup, offline state management, sync on reconnect
   - Impact on cooks-per-week: High for affected users. A single failed cook due to connectivity loss is enough to break a streak and erode trust. This is insurance against churn.
   - Recommendation: **Evaluate** — prototype the service worker for just the `/cook/[slug]` route data (no images). If it works cleanly, ship it. If sync complexity spirals, park it.
